My Goals

My goal is to build strong fundamentals while also creating concrete, high-quality artifacts.

  • Deliver clear and professional web pages with consistent design
  • Build reproducible workflows for experiments and evaluations
  • Keep improving my writing and communication for technical contexts

What “good” looks like to me

  • Readable structure, not just a page that “works”
  • Navigation that never breaks (page links + section anchors)
  • Evidence of iteration: refined layout, spacing, and content clarity

Small Research Snapshot

I’m interested in efficient and privacy-aware ML/LLM systems. A recurring theme is to balance performance with practical constraints.

Efficiency mindset

I try to think about compute/memory cost early: what is the minimal change that improves performance, and how can we validate it fairly?

Reproducibility mindset

Experiments should be comparable: clear configs, consistent baselines, and tidy logs make the results meaningful and reusable.

My Intended Learning Outcomes

From this web programming course, I aim to become more confident in building complete websites with a clean structure.

HTML structure

Write semantic and accessible HTML with clear sections and headings.

CSS layout & style

Use modern layout techniques, spacing, typography, and consistent components.

Navigation & usability

Design predictable page + section navigation and responsive behavior.

My Personal/Professional Development Planning

I keep a lightweight plan that balances coursework, research, project delivery, and well-being. The goal is steady progress, visible artifacts, and frequent reflection.

Weekly Plan (high-level)

Focus Area What I do each week How I track progress
Coursework & fundamentals Review lecture notes, complete lab/practice tasks early, and consolidate key concepts into short summaries. For web programming, I iterate on structure (HTML) first, then layout (CSS), then details (responsive + accessibility). Completed deliverables in Eclipse project folders, plus short checklists of “done / to improve”.
Research & experiments Run small, controlled experiments (one change at a time), document settings, and compare results consistently. I aim to translate findings into a clean narrative: what changed, why it matters, and what failed. Experiment logs, reproducible configs, and organized results (tables/figures) stored with version control.
Projects & portfolio Build practical demos that communicate value to others (users, teammates, stakeholders). I focus on clarity: consistent navigation, readable content sections, and professional header/footer presentation. Live demo pages + a curated Showcase page that links to each project and summarizes my role and takeaways.
Writing & communication Practice academic writing (SOP materials, summaries, and email etiquette). I rewrite key paragraphs until they are concise and logically structured. Versioned drafts and a “before/after” change log for important documents.
Health & sustainability Keep a stable routine: walking/light workouts, reasonable sleep, and short breaks during long coding or writing sessions. A simple weekly reflection note on energy level, focus, and what to adjust next week.

My cadence

I review progress at the end of each week, then set 3–5 realistic priorities for the next week. If something slips, I reduce scope rather than rushing quality.

My principle

I try to keep my plan evidence-driven: every goal should produce an artifact (a page, a report, a result table, or a clean commit history).

My Reflection and Future Directions

This course reminds me that clarity is not accidental — it comes from iteration.

When a page looks “almost right”, it usually means something is inconsistent: spacing, typography, or structure. I learned to fix the structure first, then polish styling systematically.

Going forward, I want to build more demos that feel professional: responsive navigation, consistent components, and content written for real stakeholders.
