My Blog Posts

Short notes from my coursework, research, and personal projects — mostly about building things carefully and improving them iteratively.

Building a clean multi-page website with on-page navigation

In CISC3003, I practiced structuring content for different audiences: a clear header, readable sections, and reliable anchor links. My biggest takeaway is that information architecture matters as much as visual design — a good layout reduces cognitive load.

Experiment habits that make ML work reproducible

When running training and evaluation (e.g., in PyTorch), I learned to treat logs, configs, and version control as first-class outputs. Clear naming, fixed seeds where appropriate, and concise result summaries make experiments repeatable and comparable.

Efficient fine-tuning and local deployment: why details matter

My research interests center on efficient and privacy-aware LLM workflows. Even small implementation choices — prompt templates, data splits, or rank settings in parameter-efficient fine-tuning — can meaningfully change results, so careful ablations are essential.

From “it runs” to “it feels finished”: UI polish and iteration

I enjoy refining small details: spacing, typography, consistent components, and responsive behavior. Iteration is the difference between a demo that works and a site that feels professional to users and stakeholders.
