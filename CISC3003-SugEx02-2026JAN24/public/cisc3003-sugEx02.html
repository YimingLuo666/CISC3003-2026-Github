About

I’m Charles (Yiming) Luo, a final-year Computer Science undergraduate at the University of Macau. My work focuses on building efficient, privacy-preserving, and reasoning-enhanced language models— especially practical training and deployment strategies that make advanced models more usable on real-world constraints.

I’ve participated in academic exchanges including a Winter 2025 semester at the University of Michigan–Ann Arbor and a Summer 2025 exchange at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. These experiences shaped how I approach research: I care about solid theory, measurable gains, and clean engineering that scales.

Focus

I’m interested in systems + learning methods that reduce training cost, improve reasoning, and protect user data. Recent themes I care about:

Research Themes

  • Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA) and adapter composition/merging
  • Complexity-aware training recipes (SFT vs RL allocation)
  • Model compression: pruning, evaluation-driven selection, and efficient routing
  • Privacy-preserving workflows for local / edge deployment

What I Build

  • Reproducible experiments & benchmark suites for reasoning tasks
  • Visualization dashboards (e.g., summary heatmaps for adapter metrics)
  • Clean, documented pipelines: data → training → evaluation → reporting
  • Practical engineering for research prototypes

Highlights

Efficient training · Trustworthy evaluation · Privacy-first deployment

Keywords

LoRA · RL/SFT · pruning · reasoning · edge inference
